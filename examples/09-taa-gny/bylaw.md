# BY-LAWS

## OF

## TSINGHUA ALUMNI ASSOCIATION OF GREATER NEW YORK

*A New York Nonprofit Corporation — IRS 501(c)(3)*

*Established 1979 — Incorporated as 501(c)(3): 1982*

---

## ARTICLE I — Name and Organization

**Section 1.01 — Name.** The name of this corporation is the **Tsinghua Alumni Association of Greater New York** (hereinafter referred to as "TAAGNY" or the "Association").

**Section 1.02 — Legal Status.** TAAGNY is incorporated as a nonprofit corporation in the State of New York and has held IRS 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status since 1982. Donations to TAAGNY are tax-deductible to the extent permitted by law.

**Section 1.03 — Principal Office.** The principal office of TAAGNY shall be located in New York State. The Board of Directors may change the address of the principal office as needed.

**Section 1.04 — History.** TAAGNY was established in 1979 and formally incorporated as a 501(c)(3) nonprofit in 1982, making it one of the oldest formally organized Chinese university alumni associations in the northeastern United States.

---

## ARTICLE II — Mission and Purposes

**Section 2.01.** The purposes of TAAGNY, consistent with its 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status, are:

(a) To connect and serve Tsinghua University alumni residing or working in the greater New York metropolitan area;

(b) To foster fellowship, mutual support, and professional collaboration among members;

(c) To promote the educational and cultural legacy of Tsinghua University in the New York region;

(d) To facilitate cultural exchange and mutual understanding between the United States and China;

(e) To support Tsinghua University's academic mission through advocacy, fundraising, and engagement;

(f) To assist newly arrived Tsinghua alumni and students in their transition to life in New York;

(g) To coordinate with the North America Federation of Tsinghua Alumni Associations (NAFTHAA) and other peer organizations.

**Section 2.02.** No part of the net earnings of the Association shall inure to the benefit of any member or private person. TAAGNY shall not engage in partisan political activity.

---

## ARTICLE III — Membership

**Section 3.01 — Eligibility.** The following persons are eligible for membership in TAAGNY:

(a) **Regular Members:** Any person who has graduated from, studied at, or been employed as faculty or research staff at Tsinghua University (including any school, college, or program operating under the Tsinghua University name), and who currently resides, works, or studies in the greater New York metropolitan area.

(b) **Out-of-Area Members:** Tsinghua University alumni outside the New York area may join TAAGNY as associate members, with all membership benefits except voting rights and office eligibility.

(c) **Honorary Members:** Persons who have made outstanding contributions to TAAGNY or to Tsinghua University, elected by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board. Honorary members do not pay dues.

**Section 3.02 — Dues.** Annual dues shall be set by the Board of Directors. The Board may establish reduced dues for students, recent graduates, and senior members. A life membership option may be established at the Board's discretion.

**Section 3.03 — Good Standing.** A regular member is in good standing if their current dues are paid and they are not subject to any suspension. Only members in good standing may vote, nominate, or be nominated for office.

**Section 3.04 — Annual Membership Meeting.** The Association shall hold an annual membership meeting each year, generally in the spring or early fall, for the purposes of: electing Directors; receiving the annual report; receiving the financial report; and such other business as properly comes before the membership. Notice shall be provided to all members at least twenty-one (21) days in advance.

**Section 3.05 — Special Meetings.** Special meetings of the membership may be called by the Board or by written petition of at least fifteen percent (15%) of the regular members in good standing.

---

## ARTICLE IV — Board of Directors

**Section 4.01 — Powers.** The business and affairs of TAAGNY shall be managed by the Board of Directors (the "Board"), subject to applicable New York nonprofit corporation law and these By-Laws.

**Section 4.02 — Number.** The Board shall consist of no fewer than seven (7) and no more than twenty-five (25) Directors, reflecting the Association's large membership base. The exact number shall be set by Board resolution.

**Section 4.03 — Terms.** Directors shall serve terms of two (2) years. Directors may serve no more than three (3) consecutive terms before taking at least one (1) term off.

**Section 4.04 — Election.** Directors shall be elected by the regular members at the annual membership meeting. Electronic pre-voting is permitted, with in-person confirmation at the meeting. Each member may vote for up to the number of seats open for election. The candidates with the highest vote totals shall be elected.

**Section 4.05 — Duties.** The Board shall:

(a) Govern TAAGNY in accordance with its purposes and these By-Laws;

(b) Approve the annual budget and oversee financial management;

(c) Elect officers from among the Directors;

(d) Establish and oversee committees;

(e) Present an annual report and financial report to the membership;

(f) Coordinate with NAFTHAA and other Tsinghua alumni associations.

**Section 4.06 — Meetings.** The Board shall hold at least four (4) meetings per year, including the meeting at which officers are elected. A special Board meeting may be called by the President or by any five (5) Directors. Notice shall be provided at least seven (7) days in advance. A quorum shall consist of a majority of the Directors then serving.

**Section 4.07 — Compensation.** Directors shall serve without compensation, but may be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred in the performance of their duties.

**Section 4.08 — Removal.** A Director may be removed for cause by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the remaining Directors. Cause includes, but is not limited to: absence from three (3) or more consecutive meetings without excuse; conduct detrimental to the Association; or failure to fulfill the duties of the office.

**Section 4.09 — Vacancies.** Vacancies on the Board shall be filled by appointment by the remaining Directors until the next annual election.

---

## ARTICLE V — Officers

**Section 5.01 — Composition.** The officers of TAAGNY shall be: President, First Vice President, Second Vice President (and such additional Vice Presidents as the Board may determine), Secretary, and Treasurer.

**Section 5.02 — Election.** Officers shall be elected by the Board of Directors from among the Directors at the first Board meeting following the annual membership election.

**Section 5.03 — Terms.** Officers shall serve for two-year terms concurrent with their Board term. The President may serve no more than two consecutive terms in that office.

**Section 5.04 — President.** The President shall:

(a) Serve as the chief executive of the Association;

(b) Preside at all meetings of the Board and of the membership;

(c) Represent the Association externally, including in relations with NAFTHAA and Tsinghua University;

(d) Execute the policies and decisions of the Board;

(e) Appoint committee chairs with Board approval.

**Section 5.05 — First Vice President.** The First Vice President shall assist the President and shall act as President in the President's absence or incapacity. The First Vice President shall also coordinate the Association's programs and activities.

**Section 5.06 — Additional Vice Presidents.** Additional Vice Presidents shall assist in specific functional areas as assigned by the President with Board approval (e.g., Events, Membership, Young Alumni, Professional Development).

**Section 5.07 — Secretary.** The Secretary shall keep minutes of all meetings, give required notices, maintain official records and correspondence, and manage membership records.

**Section 5.08 — Treasurer.** The Treasurer shall maintain financial records, prepare financial reports, oversee banking and financial transactions, and present the annual financial report to the Board and to the membership.

---

## ARTICLE VI — Committees

**Section 6.01.** The Board may establish standing and special committees. Standing committees shall include at minimum:

(a) **Events Committee:** Plans the annual banquet, lectures, cultural events, and social gatherings;

(b) **Membership Committee:** Manages member recruitment, dues collection, and member services;

(c) **Finance Committee:** Reviews budgets, recommends financial policies, and conducts internal financial review;

(d) **Young Alumni and Student Committee:** Organizes programs for recent graduates and students; manages mentorship programs;

(e) **Technology and Industry Networking:** Organizes networking events by industry sector;

(f) **NAFTHAA Liaison Committee:** Coordinates TAAGNY's participation in NAFTHAA activities and events.

**Section 6.02.** All members in good standing are eligible to serve on committees. Committee chairs are appointed by the President with Board approval.

---

## ARTICLE VII — Finance

**Section 7.01.** All funds shall be held in accounts in the name of "Tsinghua Alumni Association of Greater New York" at one or more federally insured financial institutions.

**Section 7.02.** All disbursements over an amount set by the Board (not less than $500) shall require the signatures of at least two (2) authorized officers.

**Section 7.03.** The fiscal year shall end on December 31 of each year.

**Section 7.04.** The Treasurer shall present a detailed financial report to the Board and to the membership at the annual meeting. The Board may engage an independent certified public accountant to conduct an annual review of the Association's financial statements.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — Relationship with NAFTHAA

**Section 8.01.** TAAGNY is a member organization of the **North America Federation of Tsinghua Alumni Associations (NAFTHAA)**. TAAGNY shall participate in NAFTHAA activities, contribute to NAFTHAA's governance, and coordinate with peer Tsinghua alumni chapters across North America.

**Section 8.02.** TAAGNY shall send at least one representative to NAFTHAA meetings and events. The TAAGNY President or their designee shall serve as TAAGNY's primary representative to NAFTHAA.

---

## ARTICLE IX — Amendments

**Section 9.01.** These By-Laws may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board of Directors at a duly held Board meeting.

**Section 9.02.** Proposed amendments shall be submitted to all Directors at least twenty-one (21) days in advance of the vote.

**Section 9.03.** Major amendments affecting fundamental governance or membership rights shall also be presented at the annual membership meeting for member input before Board adoption.

---

## ARTICLE X — Dissolution

Upon dissolution of TAAGNY, after payment or provision for all liabilities, all remaining assets shall be distributed exclusively to organizations qualifying as tax-exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, as selected by the Board, with preference for organizations serving educational or cultural exchange purposes related to China and the United States.

---

*These By-Laws were adopted by the Board of Directors of the Tsinghua Alumni Association of Greater New York.*
