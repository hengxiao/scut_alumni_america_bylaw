# BY-LAWS

## OF

## SHANGHAI JIAO TONG UNIVERSITY ALUMNI ASSOCIATION IN SILICON VALLEY

*A California Nonprofit Corporation — IRS 501(c)(3)*

*(EIN: 56-2316181)*

---

## ARTICLE I — Name and Legal Status

**Section 1.01 — Name.** The name of this organization is the **Shanghai Jiao Tong University Alumni Association in Silicon Valley** (hereinafter referred to as "SJTU-SV" or the "Association").

**Section 1.02 — Legal Status.** SJTU-SV is incorporated in the State of California as a nonprofit corporation and has been recognized by the Internal Revenue Service as a tax-exempt organization under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

**Section 1.03 — Principal Office.** The principal office of SJTU-SV shall be located in the Silicon Valley area of California. The Board of Directors may change the location of the principal office by resolution.

---

## ARTICLE II — Mission and Purposes

**Section 2.01.** The mission of SJTU-SV is to connect, engage, and support Shanghai Jiao Tong University alumni in Silicon Valley and the greater San Francisco Bay Area. The specific purposes of SJTU-SV are:

(a) To foster friendship, mutual support, and a sense of community among SJTU alumni in Silicon Valley;

(b) To facilitate professional networking, career development, and entrepreneurship among members;

(c) To provide mentorship and support for SJTU students considering opportunities in Silicon Valley and for recently arrived alumni;

(d) To promote academic and cultural exchange between Silicon Valley and Shanghai Jiao Tong University;

(e) To support SJTU's educational mission through advocacy, fundraising, and volunteer engagement;

(f) To celebrate and share SJTU's academic tradition and the contributions of SJTU alumni to Silicon Valley's technology ecosystem.

**Section 2.02.** No part of the net earnings or assets of SJTU-SV shall inure to the benefit of any member, director, officer, or private person. SJTU-SV shall not engage in partisan political activities.

---

## ARTICLE III — Membership

**Section 3.01 — Eligibility.** Membership in SJTU-SV is open to:

(a) **Regular Members:** Any person who has graduated from, studied at, or been employed as faculty or research staff at Shanghai Jiao Tong University, and who resides, works, or studies in the Silicon Valley area or greater San Francisco Bay Area.

(b) **Student Members:** Current students enrolled at Shanghai Jiao Tong University who are visiting or interning in Silicon Valley. Student members are entitled to all membership benefits but may not vote or hold office.

(c) **Associate Members:** Spouses, domestic partners, or immediate family members of regular members, or individuals with a significant connection to the SJTU community as determined by the Board. Associate members may not vote or hold office.

(d) **Honorary Members:** Individuals who have made distinguished contributions to SJTU-SV or to Shanghai Jiao Tong University, designated by two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board. Honorary members are not required to pay dues.

**Section 3.02 — Dues.** Annual membership dues shall be set by the Board of Directors and may differ by membership category. The Board may establish a life membership option. Dues shall be payable at the beginning of each calendar year.

**Section 3.03 — Good Standing.** A member is in good standing if current annual dues are paid. Only regular members in good standing may vote and hold office.

**Section 3.04 — Annual Meeting.** SJTU-SV shall hold an annual membership meeting once each year to: (i) present the annual report; (ii) present the financial report; (iii) elect Directors; and (iv) transact such other business as is properly before the membership. Notice shall be provided at least fourteen (14) days in advance.

---

## ARTICLE IV — Board of Directors

**Section 4.01 — Powers.** All corporate powers of SJTU-SV shall be exercised by or under the authority of the Board of Directors (the "Board").

**Section 4.02 — Number.** The Board shall consist of no fewer than five (5) and no more than twelve (12) Directors, all of whom must be regular members in good standing.

**Section 4.03 — Terms.** Directors shall serve two-year terms. Directors may serve up to three consecutive terms before taking at least one term off.

**Section 4.04 — Election.** Directors shall be elected by regular members in good standing at the annual membership meeting. Electronic voting is permitted, with ballots open for at least one week before the meeting.

**Section 4.05 — Duties.** The Board shall:

(a) Manage and oversee the activities of SJTU-SV in furtherance of its mission;

(b) Adopt an annual budget and oversee financial management;

(c) Appoint and oversee officers;

(d) Establish and dissolve committees;

(e) Ensure legal and tax compliance;

(f) Promote the growth and engagement of the SJTU community in Silicon Valley.

**Section 4.06 — Meetings.** The Board shall hold at least four (4) meetings per year. A special meeting may be called by the President or by any three (3) Directors. Notice shall be provided at least five (5) days in advance. Meetings may be held in person or by video conference. A quorum shall consist of a majority of Directors then serving.

**Section 4.07 — Compensation.** Directors shall serve without compensation but may be reimbursed for reasonable expenses incurred in carrying out their duties.

**Section 4.08 — Vacancies and Removal.** Vacancies shall be filled by the Board by appointment until the next annual election. A Director may be removed for cause by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the remaining Directors.

---

## ARTICLE V — Officers

**Section 5.01 — Composition.** The officers of SJTU-SV shall be: President, Vice President(s) (up to three), Secretary, and Treasurer. Officers shall be elected by the Board from among the Directors.

**Section 5.02 — President.** The President shall serve as chief executive officer of SJTU-SV, preside at all meetings of the Board and of the membership, represent the Association externally, and implement the Board's decisions and policies.

**Section 5.03 — Vice Presidents.** Vice Presidents shall assist the President and shall act as President in the President's absence or incapacity.

**Section 5.04 — Secretary.** The Secretary shall keep minutes of all meetings, give required notices, and maintain official records of the Association.

**Section 5.05 — Treasurer.** The Treasurer shall maintain financial records, prepare financial reports, oversee the annual budget, and co-sign all material financial transactions.

**Section 5.06 — Terms.** Officers shall serve for a term of two (2) years concurrent with their Board term. The President may serve no more than two consecutive terms.

---

## ARTICLE VI — Committees

**Section 6.01.** The Board may establish standing and special committees to advance the mission of SJTU-SV. Standing committees shall include at minimum:

(a) **Events and Programs Committee:** Plans and organizes social, cultural, and professional events.

(b) **Membership Committee:** Manages member recruitment, retention, and engagement.

(c) **Finance Committee:** Assists the Treasurer and recommends financial policies to the Board.

(d) **Student and Career Development Committee:** Coordinates mentorship, internship, and career support programs.

(e) **Outreach and Communications Committee:** Manages the Association's website, newsletter, social media, and public relations.

**Section 6.02.** Committees are open to all members in good standing. Committee chairs shall be appointed by the President with Board approval.

---

## ARTICLE VII — Finance

**Section 7.01.** All Association funds shall be deposited in one or more accounts held in the name of SJTU-SV at federally insured financial institutions.

**Section 7.02.** Disbursements above an amount set by the Board (not less than $200) shall require the authorization of at least two (2) officers.

**Section 7.03.** The fiscal year shall end on December 31 of each year. The Treasurer shall present an annual financial report to the Board and to the membership.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — Amendments

**Section 8.01.** These By-Laws may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the full Board of Directors at any duly noticed Board meeting.

**Section 8.02.** Proposed amendments shall be circulated to all Directors at least fourteen (14) days before the vote.

**Section 8.03.** Significant amendments affecting member rights or the fundamental purposes of the Association shall also be presented to the general membership for comment before the Board vote.

---

## ARTICLE IX — Dissolution

Upon dissolution of SJTU-SV, after payment or provision for all liabilities, all remaining assets shall be distributed to one or more organizations qualified as tax-exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, as determined by the Board, with preference for organizations whose purposes relate to education, scholarship, or Chinese-American cultural exchange.

---

*These By-Laws were adopted by the Board of Directors of the Shanghai Jiao Tong University Alumni Association in Silicon Valley.*
