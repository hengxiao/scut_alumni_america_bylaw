# CONSTITUTION AND BY-LAWS

## OF

## HARVARD CHINESE STUDENTS & SCHOLARS ASSOCIATION

*A Harvard University Recognized Student Organization*

*Amended and Restated: June 2023*

---

## ARTICLE I — Name and Purpose

**Section 1.01 — Name.** The name of this organization is the **Harvard Chinese Students & Scholars Association** (hereinafter "HCSSA").

**Section 1.02 — Purpose.** HCSSA is dedicated to promoting social, intellectual, and cultural activities for Chinese students and scholars at Harvard University and for other interested members of the Harvard community. HCSSA strives to:

(a) Provide community, friendship, and mutual support for Chinese students and scholars at Harvard;

(b) Organize academic, cultural, social, and professional events that enrich the lives of its members;

(c) Serve as a bridge between Chinese and other communities within Harvard and in the greater Boston area;

(d) Celebrate Chinese culture, language, and traditions;

(e) Support the professional and academic development of its members.

**Section 1.03 — Non-Partisan.** HCSSA is non-political and shall not engage in partisan political activity or act on behalf of any government.

---

## ARTICLE II — Membership

**Section 2.01 — Open Membership.** Membership in HCSSA is open to all students and scholars of Harvard University who are interested in HCSSA's mission and purposes, regardless of nationality, ethnicity, or background.

**Section 2.02 — Voting Eligibility.** Any HCSSA member with a valid Harvard University email address is eligible to vote in officer elections. Each eligible voter shall be entitled to one (1) anonymous ballot.

**Section 2.03 — Officer Eligibility.** Any degree-seeking Harvard University student is eligible to run for HCSSA office, subject to any applicable rules and policies of Harvard University. Officers must be enrolled students at Harvard during their entire term of service.

**Section 2.04 — Non-Discrimination.** HCSSA shall not discriminate in its membership or in its operations on the basis of race, color, national origin, ethnicity, sex, gender identity, sexual orientation, disability, religion, or any other characteristic protected by applicable law or Harvard University policy.

---

## ARTICLE III — Officers and Governance

**Section 3.01 — Executive Board.** HCSSA shall be governed by an **Executive Board** consisting of: (i) President; (ii) Vice President(s); (iii) Secretary; (iv) Treasurer; and (v) Committee Chairs.

**Section 3.02 — President.** The President shall:

(a) Serve as the chief executive officer of HCSSA;

(b) Exercise general supervision over the organization and oversee the operations of the Management Team;

(c) Represent HCSSA externally;

(d) Preside at all general meetings and Executive Board meetings;

(e) Appoint Committee Chairs in consultation with the Vice Presidents.

The President candidate must have served as an HCSSA officer for at least two (2) terms prior to standing for election. The President and Vice President must run as a ticket.

**Section 3.03 — Vice President(s).** Vice Presidents shall:

(a) Assist the President in running the organization;

(b) Assume office temporarily and schedule a new election if the President leaves office before term completion;

(c) Oversee specific functional areas as assigned by the President.

The Vice President candidate must have served as an HCSSA officer for at least one (1) term prior to standing for election.

**Section 3.04 — Secretary.** The Secretary shall:

(a) Take minutes of all HCSSA general meetings and Executive Board meetings;

(b) Keep official documents and records of the organization;

(c) Maintain official membership lists and communications records;

(d) Handle official correspondence of the organization.

**Section 3.05 — Treasurer.** The Treasurer shall:

(a) Maintain financial records of the organization;

(b) Raise and apply for funding through various channels;

(c) Co-sign all checks with the President on behalf of the organization;

(d) Present financial reports at general meetings and to Harvard University as required;

(e) Prepare and manage the annual budget.

**Section 3.06 — Committee Chairs and Fellows.** Committee Chairs shall:

(a) Centrally organize and coordinate activities related to their committee's responsibilities;

(b) Report timely to the President and Vice Presidents on committee activities and plans;

(c) Recruit and manage committee volunteers;

(d) Be appointed by the President in consultation with Vice Presidents.

No person shall serve as Chair of more than one (1) committee simultaneously. Officers may participate in more than one committee.

---

## ARTICLE IV — Committees

**Section 4.01 — Establishment of Committees.** HCSSA shall operate through committees. New committees may be added and existing committees removed by majority vote at a general meeting, after being proposed by the Executive Board.

**Section 4.02 — Standing Committees.** Standing committees may include:

(a) **Events Committee:** Plans and organizes social events, cultural celebrations, and community gatherings;

(b) **Academic Committee:** Organizes lectures, seminars, and academic exchange programs;

(c) **Career and Professional Development Committee:** Organizes career fairs, networking events, and mentorship programs;

(d) **Cultural and Arts Committee:** Promotes Chinese culture, arts, and traditions;

(e) **Community Service Committee:** Coordinates community outreach and service programs;

(f) **Communications Committee:** Manages HCSSA's website, social media, newsletter, and publicity.

**Section 4.03 — Volunteers.** Additional volunteers are welcome to join any committee. Committee volunteers shall not be recognized as official HCSSA officers and are not subject to officer-specific policies, but their contributions shall be acknowledged.

---

## ARTICLE V — Elections

**Section 5.01 — Annual Election.** Officers shall be elected annually, before the end of the spring semester (generally in late April or May), by eligible members of HCSSA. New officers shall commence duties immediately upon election.

**Section 5.02 — Voting Procedure.** Elections shall be conducted by secret ballot. Each eligible voter shall be entitled to one vote per contested office.

**Section 5.03 — Majority Requirement.** Candidates for President/Vice President ticket must receive a majority (more than 50%) of valid votes cast to be elected. If no candidate receives a majority, a runoff shall be held between the two tickets with the highest vote totals.

**Section 5.04 — Other Offices.** Candidates for other officer positions (Secretary, Treasurer, Committee Chairs) must receive a majority of votes cast for their respective positions.

**Section 5.05 — Vacancies.** In the event of a vacancy in any officer position, the Executive Board shall nominate a replacement for majority approval by HCSSA members at a general meeting.

---

## ARTICLE VI — Meetings

**Section 6.01 — General Meetings.** HCSSA shall hold general meetings at intervals determined by the Executive Board, generally at least three (3) times per academic semester.

**Section 6.02 — Executive Board Meetings.** The Executive Board shall meet at least monthly during the academic year.

**Section 6.03 — Notice.** Notice of general meetings shall be provided to all members at least five (5) days in advance by email or other electronic communication.

**Section 6.04 — Quorum.** A quorum for general meetings shall consist of those members present. A quorum for Executive Board meetings shall consist of a majority of the officers then serving.

---

## ARTICLE VII — Finance

**Section 7.01.** HCSSA shall maintain financial accounts as authorized by Harvard University's student organization financial policies.

**Section 7.02.** The Treasurer and President are jointly authorized to sign checks and authorize expenditures on behalf of HCSSA, subject to any Harvard University policies.

**Section 7.03.** HCSSA shall submit required financial reports to Harvard University's student organization office on schedule.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — Amendments

**Section 8.01.** This Constitution and By-Laws may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the Executive Board, ratified by a majority of members present at a duly noticed general meeting.

**Section 8.02.** Proposed amendments shall be circulated to all members at least seven (7) days before the meeting at which the ratification vote will occur.

---

## ARTICLE IX — Compliance with Harvard University Policies

**Section 9.01.** HCSSA shall comply with all applicable Harvard University policies governing recognized student organizations, including but not limited to non-discrimination policies, financial management requirements, and event safety standards.

**Section 9.02.** In the event of any conflict between this Constitution and Harvard University policy, Harvard University policy shall govern.

---

*This Constitution and By-Laws of the Harvard Chinese Students & Scholars Association was amended and restated in June 2023.*
