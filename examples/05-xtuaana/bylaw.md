# BY-LAWS

## OF

## XIANGTAN UNIVERSITY ALUMNI ASSOCIATION OF NORTH AMERICA

*A California Nonprofit Public Benefit Corporation*

---

## ARTICLE I — Name

**Section 1.** The official name of this organization shall be **Xiangtan University Alumni Association of North America** (hereinafter referred to as "XTUAANA" or the "Association").

**Section 2.** This Organization is incorporated in the State of California as a Nonprofit Public Benefit Corporation.

---

## ARTICLE II — Offices

**Section 1.** The principal executive office of the Association shall be located in the State of California. The Board of Directors shall have the authority to change the location of the principal executive office by resolution.

---

## ARTICLE III — Purposes

**Section 1.** The purposes of this Association are:

(a) To promote communication and friendship among Xiangtan University alumni in North America;

(b) To promote professional development and personal growth for the members;

(c) To provide and create opportunities for the members to share and strengthen their common interests and bonds;

(d) To enhance the connection between the members and Xiangtan University; and

(e) To help and support the development of Xiangtan University and increase its worldwide reputation.

**Section 2.** This Organization is not organized for profit and no part of its assets shall inure to the benefit of any member, but shall be used solely for the foregoing purposes. No member shall be compensated for his or her services except for expenses approved by the Board of Directors.

**Section 3.** This Organization shall not participate in any political activities or other activities that may jeopardize its tax-exempt status.

---

## ARTICLE IV — Memberships

**Section 1: Qualifications**

An individual who worked or studied at Xiangtan University and currently lives or works in North America may become a member of this Organization.

**Section 2: Rights, Privileges, and Obligations**

(a) All members have the right to elect or to be elected as members of the Board of Directors.

(b) All members may enjoy all benefits sponsored by this Organization.

(c) All members shall follow the By-Laws set forth by the Organization and the decisions passed by the Board of Directors.

**Section 3: Membership Fees**

A member shall pay a membership fee as determined by the Board of Directors from time to time.

**Section 4: Membership Meeting**

Regular membership meetings shall be held once a year. Special membership meetings may be called by a majority of the Board of Directors.

Notice of meetings shall be served by the Secretary at least one (1) week prior to the date of the meeting. Notice shall be in writing or in the form of electronic mail stating the purpose, date, and place.

The purposes of the membership meeting are:

(a) To amend the By-Laws;

(b) To elect directors of the Board of Directors, or recall directors and/or officers;

(c) To discuss the Organization's activities, and to review the work of the Board of Directors in the past year;

(d) To discuss other important business.

---

## ARTICLE V — Board of Directors

**Section 1: Powers**

The governing powers of this Organization shall be vested in a Board of Directors.

**Section 2: Number**

The minimum number of board members shall be eleven (11), and the maximum number of board members shall be twenty-one (21).

**Section 3: Duties**

(a) To manage the activities of the Organization and to uphold the rules and regulations consistent with the By-Laws.

(b) To cause a record to be kept of its meetings and to cause a full statement to be presented in writing at the annual membership meeting showing, in detail, the assets and liabilities of the Organization.

(c) To call membership meetings.

(d) To appoint executive officers.

(e) To plan various activities.

(f) To undertake all actions necessary to fulfill the purposes of the Organization.

**Section 4: Meetings**

(a) There shall be at least one regular Board meeting per year. Special meetings of the Board for any purpose may be called at any time by the Chairman, any Vice Chairman, the Secretary, or by any five (5) members of the Board.

(b) Notice of Meetings shall be served by the Secretary at least one (1) week prior to the date of the meeting.

(c) Business can be lawfully transacted if a quorum consisting of the majority of the authorized number of directors is in attendance. If a quorum is present at a duly held meeting, every act or decision done or made by a majority of the directors present shall be the act of the Board of Directors.

(d) For electing the Chairman, Vice Chairmen, appointing Officers, and for other issues that the Board has designated as important matters, a majority of the authorized board members is required to pass a vote.

(e) Each board meeting shall have minute records. A summary of each board meeting shall be accessible by Organization members.

**Section 5: Term of Office**

Each Director shall hold office for three (3) years, or until such Director's successor is elected and qualified under these By-Laws.

**Section 6: Nomination**

Any member may be nominated by a method of nomination authorized by the Board of Directors.

**Section 7: Election**

(a) The Directors shall be elected by the members. A Director candidate shall be a member.

(b) Each candidate shall submit a brief biography for election purposes.

(c) Voting may be held electronically. Each member shall submit their vote by the designated deadline.

(d) Each candidate who receives a majority of votes cast is elected. If the number of candidates so elected is less than the minimum required number of directors, the candidates with the highest vote totals shall be elected until the minimum is reached. If the number of candidates elected exceeds the maximum, the candidates with the same lowest vote total shall draw lots to determine who is elected.

**Section 8: Vacancies in the Board of Directors**

(a) A Director may resign upon giving written notice to the Chairman, any Vice Chairman, or the Secretary.

(b) A Director who is absent from Board meetings for three (3) consecutive meetings without excuse is considered to have resigned from the Board.

(c) If the number of Board members falls below the minimum due to resignation or other reasons, temporary board members shall be elected within six (6) months by the remaining Board members to restore the Board to the minimum.

---

## ARTICLE VI — Executive Officers

**Section 1: Composition and Term of Office**

The Executive Officers shall consist of one (1) Chairman, up to six (6) Vice Chairmen, one (1) Secretary, and one (1) Treasurer, each serving for a term of three (3) years.

**Section 2: Chairman of Board of Directors**

(a) The Chairman shall execute the directives and policies of the Board and shall provide leadership to achieve the purposes of the Organization.

(b) The Chairman shall preside at all meetings of the Board and of the Membership.

(c) The Chairman shall represent the Organization externally.

(d) The Chairman shall be the chief executive officer of the Organization.

The Chairman shall be elected by the Board of Directors. A candidate receiving a majority of the Board's votes is elected. If no candidate receives a majority, the top two vote-getters advance to a runoff; the candidate receiving more votes in the runoff is elected. Ties shall be resolved by lot.

The service of a Chairman may be terminated before the end of the three-year term if the Chairman resigns or if a minimum of one-third (1/3) of the Board members propose a re-election against him or her.

**Section 3: Vice Chairmen**

Vice Chairmen shall exercise the Chairman's duties when the Chairman is absent. Vice Chairmen shall be elected by the Board by the same process as the Chairman.

**Section 4: Secretary**

The Secretary shall:

(a) Keep a full and complete record of the proceedings of the members and the Board of Directors;

(b) Keep the seal of the corporation and affix it to such papers and instruments as may be required;

(c) Make service of notices as necessary or proper;

(d) Discharge other duties as pertained to the office or prescribed by the Board.

The Secretary shall be appointed by the Board of Directors for a term of three (3) years.

**Section 5: Treasurer**

(a) The Treasurer shall collect all dues and donations and shall keep account of all assets and liabilities.

(b) The Treasurer shall maintain accurate accounts of all financial transactions.

(c) The Treasurer is authorized to co-sign checks and disburse funds for all obligations approved by the Board.

(d) The Treasurer shall submit an annual account of the Organization.

(e) The Treasurer shall be custodian of all bank records and financial accounts.

The Treasurer shall be appointed by the Board of Directors for a term of three (3) years.

---

## ARTICLE VII — Organization Fund

The Organization's funds shall be deposited in a federally insured financial institution under the name of Xiangtan University Alumni Association of North America. Two authorized officers' signatures shall be required for any withdrawal.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — Amendments of By-Laws

**Section 1: Procedure**

The By-Laws shall be amended in the following manner:

(a) Proposal in writing;

(b) Submittal and discussion;

(c) Adoption by a two-thirds majority vote.

**Section 2: Proposal**

An amendment shall be proposed in writing and signed by at least ten percent (10%) of the members, or by at least six (6) members of the Board of Directors.

**Section 3: Submittal and Discussion**

A proposed amendment shall be announced thirty (30) days prior to the annual membership meeting and shall be presented for discussion at that meeting.

**Section 4: Adoption**

Adoption shall be by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the members present at the membership meeting at which the amendment is presented.

---

*These By-Laws were adopted by the Board of Directors of the Xiangtan University Alumni Association of North America.*
