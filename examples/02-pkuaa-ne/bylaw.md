# BY-LAWS

## OF

## PEKING UNIVERSITY ALUMNI ASSOCIATION OF NEW ENGLAND, INC.

*A Massachusetts Nonprofit Corporation — IRS 501(c)(3)*

---

## ARTICLE I — Name and Legal Status

**Section 1.01 — Name.** The name of this corporation is the **Peking University Alumni Association of New England, Inc.** (hereinafter "PKUAA-NE" or the "Corporation").

**Section 1.02 — Nonprofit Status.** PKUAA-NE is organized and operated exclusively for charitable, religious, educational, and/or scientific purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended. No part of the net earnings of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its members, directors, officers, or other private persons.

**Section 1.03 — Non-Partisan.** PKUAA-NE shall not participate in or intervene in any political campaign on behalf of (or in opposition to) any candidate for public office.

---

## ARTICLE II — Principal Office

**Section 2.01.** The principal office of PKUAA-NE shall be located in the Commonwealth of Massachusetts. The Board of Directors may change the location of the principal office from time to time.

---

## ARTICLE III — Purposes

**Section 3.01.** The specific purposes of PKUAA-NE are:

(a) To promote cultural understanding and educational exchanges between the peoples of the United States and the People's Republic of China;

(b) To build and strengthen the Peking University alumni network in New England;

(c) To support the academic and professional development of Peking University alumni residing or studying in the New England region;

(d) To assist newly arrived alumni and students in their transition to life in the United States;

(e) To organize scholarly, cultural, and social programs and events for the benefit of members and the broader community;

(f) To further the educational mission and global reputation of Peking University.

**Section 3.02.** All activities of PKUAA-NE shall be consistent with its status as an organization exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

---

## ARTICLE IV — Membership

**Section 4.01 — Eligibility.** The following categories of persons are eligible for membership in PKUAA-NE:

(a) **Regular Members:** Any person who has graduated from, studied at, or been employed as faculty at Peking University, and who resides, works, or studies in the New England region (Massachusetts, Connecticut, Rhode Island, Vermont, New Hampshire, or Maine).

(b) **Honorary Members:** Any person who has made outstanding contributions to PKUAA-NE or to Peking University, upon nomination by the Board and approval by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Board of Directors. Honorary members are not entitled to vote.

**Section 4.02 — Membership Dues.** Regular members shall pay annual dues in an amount determined by the Board of Directors. The Board may establish a life membership option. Students actively enrolled at a college or university may be eligible for reduced dues as determined by the Board.

**Section 4.03 — Members in Good Standing.** A member is in good standing if the member's current annual dues are paid and the member is not under any suspension imposed by the Board.

**Section 4.04 — Rights of Regular Members.** Each regular member in good standing is entitled to:

(a) One vote at any general membership meeting;

(b) Nominate and be nominated as a candidate for the Board of Directors;

(c) Participate in all activities and programs of PKUAA-NE;

(d) Access membership directories and communications, subject to applicable privacy policies.

**Section 4.05 — Annual Membership Meeting.** An annual meeting of members shall be held once each year for the purposes of: (i) electing Directors; (ii) receiving the annual financial report; (iii) discussing the work and plans of the Corporation. Notice of the annual meeting shall be given to all members at least twenty-one (21) days in advance, specifying the date, time, location, and agenda.

**Section 4.06 — Special Meetings.** Special meetings of members may be called by the Board of Directors or by written petition signed by at least twenty percent (20%) of members in good standing.

---

## ARTICLE V — Board of Directors

**Section 5.01 — Powers.** The business and affairs of PKUAA-NE shall be managed by or under the direction of the Board of Directors (the "Board"), subject to applicable Massachusetts nonprofit corporation law, these By-Laws, and any applicable federal law.

**Section 5.02 — Number and Composition.** The Board shall consist of no fewer than five (5) and no more than fifteen (15) Directors, all of whom must be regular members in good standing. The exact number within this range shall be determined by resolution of the Board.

**Section 5.03 — Terms of Office.** Directors shall serve terms of two (2) years. Directors may serve no more than three (3) consecutive terms. After a gap of at least one (1) term, former Directors are again eligible for election.

**Section 5.04 — Election.** Directors shall be elected by the regular members at the annual membership meeting. Each member may vote for up to the number of seats open for election. The candidates receiving the highest vote totals shall be elected. In the event of a tie for the final seat, a runoff vote shall be conducted at the same meeting.

**Section 5.05 — Duties of the Board.**

(a) Govern the Corporation in accordance with its purposes and these By-Laws;

(b) Oversee the financial integrity and legal compliance of the Corporation;

(c) Approve the annual budget and financial reports;

(d) Appoint and oversee officers;

(e) Establish and dissolve committees as needed;

(f) Ensure that the Corporation's charitable purposes are being served.

**Section 5.06 — Meetings of the Board.** The Board shall meet at least three (3) times per year. A special meeting of the Board may be called by the President or by any three (3) Directors. Notice shall be provided at least seven (7) days in advance. A quorum shall consist of a majority of the Directors then in office. Actions shall require a majority vote of Directors present, except as otherwise specified.

**Section 5.07 — Action Without Meeting.** The Board may take action without a meeting if all Directors consent in writing (including by email) to the action to be taken. Such written consent shall be filed with the minutes of the Board.

**Section 5.08 — Removal.** A Director may be removed by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the remaining Directors for cause, including but not limited to: failure to attend three (3) or more consecutive Board meetings without excuse; conduct detrimental to the Corporation; or violation of these By-Laws.

**Section 5.09 — Vacancies.** Vacancies on the Board shall be filled by appointment by the remaining Directors until the next annual election, at which point the seat shall be filled for a new full term.

---

## ARTICLE VI — Officers

**Section 6.01 — Composition.** The officers of PKUAA-NE shall be: President, Vice President(s) (up to three), Secretary, and Treasurer. Officers shall be elected by the Board of Directors from among the Directors.

**Section 6.02 — Terms.** Officers shall serve for a term of two (2) years, concurrent with their Board term. No person may serve as President for more than two consecutive terms.

**Section 6.03 — President.** The President shall:

(a) Be the chief executive officer of the Corporation;

(b) Preside at all meetings of the Board and of the membership;

(c) Represent the Corporation externally;

(d) Execute all decisions and directives of the Board;

(e) Appoint committee chairs with Board approval.

**Section 6.04 — Vice President(s).** Vice Presidents shall assist the President in the discharge of duties and shall act as President in the President's absence or incapacity.

**Section 6.05 — Secretary.** The Secretary shall keep minutes of all meetings, give required notices, maintain official records, and perform such other duties as assigned by the Board.

**Section 6.06 — Treasurer.** The Treasurer shall maintain accurate financial records, present financial reports to the Board and to the membership, and oversee all banking and financial transactions of the Corporation in accordance with the budget approved by the Board.

---

## ARTICLE VII — Finances and Fiscal Management

**Section 7.01.** All funds shall be deposited in accounts held in the name of "Peking University Alumni Association of New England, Inc." at one or more federally insured financial institutions selected by the Board.

**Section 7.02.** All disbursements exceeding an amount set by the Board (not less than $500) shall require the signatures of at least two authorized officers.

**Section 7.03.** The fiscal year of the Corporation shall end on December 31 of each year.

**Section 7.04.** The Treasurer shall prepare an annual financial report and present it to the Board and to the membership at the annual meeting. The Board may engage an independent accountant to review the Corporation's financial statements.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — Committees

**Section 8.01.** The Board may establish standing committees and special committees to carry out the Corporation's purposes. Committees shall be accountable to the Board and shall have no authority to act on behalf of the Corporation without Board approval, unless specifically authorized.

**Section 8.02.** Membership on committees shall be open to any member in good standing. Committee chairs shall be appointed by the President with Board approval.

---

## ARTICLE IX — Indemnification and Limitation of Liability

**Section 9.01.** PKUAA-NE shall indemnify its Directors and officers to the fullest extent permitted by Massachusetts law against liabilities and expenses arising from their service to the Corporation, provided such persons acted in good faith.

**Section 9.02.** No Director shall be personally liable to the Corporation for monetary damages for breach of their duty of care as a Director to the extent that Massachusetts law permits the limitation or elimination of such liability.

---

## ARTICLE X — Amendments

**Section 10.01.** These By-Laws may be altered, amended, or repealed by the approval of:

(i) Two-thirds (2/3) of the Board of Directors at a duly held Board meeting; or

(ii) A majority of the regular members in good standing, voting at a duly noticed annual or special membership meeting.

**Section 10.02.** Proposed amendments must be circulated to all Directors and to the general membership at least twenty-one (21) days before the meeting at which the vote will be taken.

---

## ARTICLE XI — Dissolution

Upon dissolution of PKUAA-NE, after all obligations and liabilities have been paid or adequately provided for, the remaining assets shall be distributed to one or more organizations that are organized and operated exclusively for charitable or educational purposes and that qualify as exempt organizations under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, as determined by the Board of Directors.

---

*These By-Laws were adopted by the Board of Directors of the Peking University Alumni Association of New England, Inc.*
