# BY-LAWS

## OF

## CHIAO TUNG UNIVERSITY ALUMNI ASSOCIATION IN AMERICA

*A National Federation of Alumni Chapters*

---

## ARTICLE I — Name and Objectives

**Section 1: Name**

The name of this association is the **Chiao Tung University Alumni Association in America** (hereinafter referred to as "CTUAAA" or the "Association").

**Section 2: Objectives**

The objectives of CTUAAA are:

(a) To develop the unity of spirit and camaraderie among alumni of all five Chiao Tung Universities in America;

(b) To initiate and charter Chapters of CTUAAA at different locations throughout North America;

(c) To coordinate activities and programs common to all Chapters;

(d) To help all Chapters carry out their individual objectives;

(e) To promote the further advancement and worldwide reputation of the Chiao Tung Universities;

(f) To authorize and coordinate alumni reunions approximately every five (5) years.

**Section 3: Non-Partisan Character**

CTUAAA is a non-political, non-religious, and non-profit organization. It shall not participate in any partisan political activity.

**Section 4: Chiao Tung Universities**

For purposes of these By-Laws, the "Chiao Tung Universities" refers collectively to:
- Shanghai Jiao Tong University (SJTU), Shanghai, China
- Xi'an Jiaotong University (XJTU), Xi'an, China
- Southwestern Jiaotong University (SWJTU), Chengdu, China
- Beijing Jiaotong University (BJTU), Beijing, China
- National Yang Ming Chiao Tung University (NYCU), Hsinchu, Taiwan

---

## ARTICLE II — Organization and Membership

**Section 1: Federation Structure**

CTUAAA is a federation of Chapters throughout America, each of which shares the same objectives as CTUAAA. Each Chapter may organize into Branches as appropriate to its geographic area.

**Section 2: Chapter Membership Requirements**

Each Chapter shall have a minimum of thirty (30) members to be recognized and chartered as a Chapter of CTUAAA. Individual members of all Chapters are thereby members of CTUAAA.

**Section 3: Individual Eligibility**

Any graduate or faculty member of any of the five Chiao Tung Universities may become a member of the Chapter serving their area of residence or work in North America.

**Section 4: Reunion Participation**

Persons eligible to attend a Reunion of CTUAAA shall include: members and their family members; current students, graduates, professors, and advisers of any of the five Chiao Tung Universities; and special guests as determined by the Board.

---

## ARTICLE III — Board of Directors

**Section 1: Powers**

The **Board of Directors** shall be the governing and policy-making body of CTUAAA.

**Section 2: Composition**

The Board shall consist of all Directors duly elected by the Officers of each Chapter. The representation formula shall be:

(a) One (1) Director for a Chapter with thirty (30) to one hundred forty-nine (149) members;

(b) Two (2) Directors for a Chapter with one hundred fifty (150) or more members.

**Section 3: Chairperson**

The Board shall elect a Chairperson of the Board from among its members. The Chairperson shall preside at all Board meetings and shall serve as the principal representative of CTUAAA between national events.

**Section 4: Term**

Directors shall serve for a term established by their respective Chapters, generally two (2) years. The Chairperson shall serve for a term of two (2) years.

**Section 5: Meetings**

(a) The Board shall hold at least one (1) meeting per year, which may be held in conjunction with national events.

(b) Notice of Board meetings shall be provided to all Directors at least thirty (30) days in advance.

(c) A quorum shall consist of a majority of Directors then serving.

(d) Actions shall require a majority vote of Directors present at a duly noticed meeting with quorum, except where a two-thirds (2/3) vote is required.

**Section 6: Voting**

Unless otherwise specified in these By-Laws, all Board votes shall be by simple majority of Directors present. A two-thirds (2/3) majority of the full Board is required to:

(a) Replace the President or Vice-President of a Reunion;

(b) Amend these By-Laws;

(c) Revoke or suspend a Chapter charter.

---

## ARTICLE IV — Chapters

**Section 1: Charter**

A group of thirty (30) or more eligible alumni in a geographic region may petition the Board for recognition as a Chapter of CTUAAA. The Board shall evaluate the petition and may grant or deny the charter.

**Section 2: Chapter By-Laws**

Each Chapter shall adopt its own by-laws governing its internal affairs. Chapter by-laws shall not conflict with these National By-Laws. Chapter by-laws shall be submitted to the Board for review upon adoption or amendment.

**Section 3: Chapter Autonomy**

Within the constraints of these National By-Laws, each Chapter shall have autonomy to organize its own activities, elect its own officers, and manage its own finances.

**Section 4: Chapter Obligations**

Each Chapter shall:

(a) Maintain accurate membership records and report membership counts annually to CTUAAA;

(b) Send the appropriate number of Directors to the Board;

(c) Cooperate with other Chapters in joint activities and national events;

(d) Contribute to national reunion planning as requested.

**Section 5: Example — Houston Chapter**

As an illustrative model, Chapter by-law amendments at the local level shall be proposed by any member, endorsed by at least ten (10) members, studied by the Chapter's Executive Committee, and brought to a general chapter meeting within three (3) months of receipt of the proposal.

---

## ARTICLE V — Reunions

**Section 1: Frequency**

National Reunions of CTUAAA shall be held approximately every five (5) years.

**Section 2: Reunion Leadership**

The Board shall elect a President and Vice-President of the Reunion at a Board meeting held at least eighteen (18) months prior to the scheduled date of the Reunion. Candidates must be active members.

**Section 3: Replacement**

The Board may, by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the full Board, replace the Reunion President or Vice-President at any time without cause.

**Section 4: Budget**

The Reunion President shall prepare a detailed budget of all expenses and projected income, including a guest list. The budget shall be submitted to and approved by the Board Chairperson at least six (6) months prior to the Reunion.

---

## ARTICLE VI — Chiao Tung University Alumni Foundation of America, Inc.

**Section 1.** The **Chiao Tung University Alumni Foundation of America, Inc. (CTUAFA)** is a separate legal entity, duly incorporated as a tax-exempt non-profit organization under the laws of the United States.

**Section 2.** CTUAFA's purposes include:

(a) To aid the Chiao Tung Universities in financing their educational activities;

(b) To aid alumni, faculty, and their immediate family members in the pursuit of higher education in the United States and China.

**Section 3.** CTUAFA is governed by its own separate By-Laws. CTUAAA and CTUAFA shall cooperate in furtherance of their shared mission of supporting the Chiao Tung University community.

---

## ARTICLE VII — Finances

**Section 1.** CTUAAA shall maintain a national treasury in a federally insured financial institution in the name of "Chiao Tung University Alumni Association in America."

**Section 2.** Each Chapter shall maintain its own separate treasury in its own name.

**Section 3.** National disbursements shall require the signatures of at least two (2) authorized Board officers.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — Amendments

**Section 1.** These By-Laws may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the full Board of Directors at any duly noticed Board meeting.

**Section 2.** Proposed amendments must be submitted to all Directors at least thirty (30) days before the meeting at which the vote will be taken.

**Section 3.** Amended By-Laws shall be distributed to all Chapters within thirty (30) days of adoption.

---

*These By-Laws have been adopted by the Board of Directors of the Chiao Tung University Alumni Association in America.*
