# AMENDED AND RESTATED BY-LAWS

## OF

## UNIVERSITY OF SCIENCE AND TECHNOLOGY OF CHINA ALUMNI FOUNDATION

*A Delaware Nonprofit Corporation — IRS 501(c)(3)*

*Adopted November 17, 2016*

---

## ARTICLE I — Organization

**Section 1.01 — Name.** The name of this corporation is the **University of Science and Technology of China Alumni Foundation** (hereinafter the "Corporation" or "USTCAF").

**Section 1.02 — Nature.** The Corporation is non-profit in nature and is not controlled by, or affiliated with, the University of Science and Technology of China (USTC) or any USTC alumni association. The Corporation operates as an independent organization for the exclusive purpose of fulfilling its charitable mission.

**Section 1.03 — Mission.** The Corporation is founded to:

(a) Promote the excellence of the USTC community, including students, teachers, and alumni worldwide; and

(b) Promote academic and cultural exchange between USTC and researchers and institutions worldwide.

**Section 1.04 — Tax-Exempt Status.** The Corporation is organized and operated exclusively for charitable, scientific, and educational purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986, as amended.

**Section 1.05 — All-Volunteer Organization.** The Corporation shall operate as an All Volunteer Organization (AVO). No staff shall receive compensation for services. Operating costs shall be maintained below one percent (1%) of total revenues. Substantially all donations shall be directed to programs advancing the Corporation's mission.

**Section 1.06 — Non-Distribution Constraint.** No part of the net earnings of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of any private person, including any member, director, or officer.

---

## ARTICLE II — Membership

**Section 2.01 — Members.** The members of the Corporation shall be those persons who satisfy all of the membership qualifications as determined by the Board (the "Governing Board" or "GB") from time to time. Qualifications effective as of the adoption date of these By-Laws are as set forth in Exhibit A (Membership Qualifications).

**Section 2.02 — Membership Meetings.**

(a) An annual meeting for the election of directors shall be held each year at a time and place determined by the Governing Board.

(b) A special meeting may be called at any time by resolution of the Governing Board.

(c) Notice of any membership meeting shall be given to all members at least fourteen (14) days in advance by email or other electronic communication.

**Section 2.03 — Voting.** Each member in good standing shall be entitled to one vote at any membership meeting. Voting may be conducted electronically.

---

## ARTICLE III — Governing Board

**Section 3.01 — Powers.** All corporate powers shall be exercised by or under the authority of the Governing Board (GB). The GB shall have full authority to manage the Corporation's activities, finances, and affairs, subject to these By-Laws and applicable Delaware law.

**Section 3.02 — Number.** The GB shall consist of no more than twenty (20) members (directors). The exact number shall be determined by the GB from time to time. A minimum quorum of five (5) GB members shall be required to transact any business.

**Section 3.03 — Term.** Each GB member shall serve a one-year term. GB members may be re-elected for additional terms. The transition of the GB shall occur at the annual membership meeting.

**Section 3.04 — Election.** GB members shall be elected by the members at the annual membership meeting. Candidates must be members of the Corporation in good standing. Election shall be by plurality vote of the members present.

**Section 3.05 — Meetings.** The GB shall meet at least twice per year. Special meetings may be called by the Executive Committee or by any five (5) GB members. Notice shall be provided at least seven (7) days in advance. A quorum shall consist of a majority of the GB members then serving.

**Section 3.06 — Action by Consent.** The GB may take action without a meeting by unanimous written consent of all GB members in good standing.

**Section 3.07 — Vacancies.** Vacancies on the GB may be filled by appointment by the Executive Committee for the remainder of the term.

**Section 3.08 — Removal.** A GB member may be removed by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the remaining GB members for cause, including conduct detrimental to the Corporation or repeated failure to participate in GB activities.

---

## ARTICLE IV — Executive Committee

**Section 4.01 — Composition.** The Corporation shall have a three-member **Executive Committee (EC)** consisting of a Chairperson, a Vice-Chairperson, and a Secretary.

**Section 4.02 — Election.** The EC members shall be elected by the Governing Board from among the GB members. EC members must receive a majority vote of the GB to be elected.

**Section 4.03 — Term.** EC members serve for a one-year term concurrent with the GB term in which they are elected.

**Section 4.04 — Chairperson.** The Chairperson shall:

(a) Serve as the chief executive of the Corporation;

(b) Represent the Corporation externally;

(c) Preside at all meetings of the GB and of the membership;

(d) Approve budgets submitted by standing committees prior to GB review;

(e) Approve the Reunion budget at least six months before any reunion event.

**Section 4.05 — Vice-Chairperson.** The Vice-Chairperson shall assist the Chairperson and shall act as Chairperson in the Chairperson's absence or incapacity.

**Section 4.06 — Secretary.** The Secretary shall keep minutes of all GB and EC meetings, give required notices, maintain official records, and ensure timely communications to members.

**Section 4.07 — EC Authority.** The EC shall:

(a) Supervise the management and day-to-day operations of the Corporation;

(b) Represent USTCAF externally between GB meetings;

(c) Resolve urgent matters that cannot wait for a full GB meeting, subject to subsequent GB ratification;

(d) Set the agenda for GB meetings.

---

## ARTICLE V — Standing Committees

**Section 5.01.** The Corporation shall maintain the following standing committees:

(a) **Membership and Alumni Services Committee:** Manages membership records, outreach, and alumni services.

(b) **Fund-Raising Committee:** Organizes fundraising campaigns and donor relations.

(c) **Finance Committee:** Oversees the Corporation's budget, financial controls, and annual financial report.

(d) **Publicity and Communication Committee:** Manages the Corporation's website, newsletter, and public communications.

(e) **USTC Liaison Committee:** Coordinates with USTC's administration and with USTC alumni associations in North America.

(f) **Scholarship Committee:** Manages the scholarship award process, selects recipients, and disburses scholarship funds.

**Section 5.02.** Each standing committee shall be led by a Committee Chair appointed by the Chairperson with GB approval. Committee members may be drawn from the membership at large.

**Section 5.03.** Each committee shall submit a written annual report and proposed budget to the GB.

---

## ARTICLE VI — Finance

**Section 6.01.** All funds shall be deposited in accounts held in the name of "University of Science and Technology of China Alumni Foundation" at one or more federally insured financial institutions.

**Section 6.02.** Disbursements shall require the signatures (or electronic authorization) of at least two authorized members of the EC or Finance Committee.

**Section 6.03.** The Finance Committee shall prepare an annual financial report and present it to the GB and to the membership. The GB may engage an independent accountant or auditor.

**Section 6.04.** The fiscal year of the Corporation shall end on December 31 of each year.

---

## ARTICLE VII — Amendments

**Section 7.01 — Procedure.** These By-Laws may be amended by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the Governing Board at a duly noticed GB meeting, subject to the restrictions in Section 7.02.

**Section 7.02 — Effective Date.** No amendment to these By-Laws shall become effective until thirty (30) days after its adoption by the GB.

**Section 7.03 — Restrictions.** To preserve the independence and mission of USTCAF:

(a) Article I, Sections 1.01 (Name), 1.02 (Nature), and 1.03 (Mission) shall never be amended in a manner that changes their essential meaning or the Corporation's independence.

(b) No amendment may authorize the Corporation to affiliate with, or accept control by, any university, government, or other organization.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — Dissolution

Upon dissolution of the Corporation, after payment or adequate provision for all liabilities, all remaining assets shall be distributed exclusively to one or more organizations qualifying as tax-exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, selected by the GB, with preference given to organizations whose purposes are substantially similar to those of USTCAF.

---

## EXHIBIT A — Membership Qualifications

*(As adopted November 17, 2016; may be amended by GB resolution)*

A person is a member of the Corporation at any given time if that person meets **all** of the following conditions:

(1) **Financial Contribution:** The person has, in the preceding 365 days:
   - Made a donation pledge of twenty dollars ($20) or more and remitted payment within thirty (30) days of making the pledge; **OR**
   - Paid annual membership dues of ten dollars ($10); **OR**
   - Been previously designated as a "lifetime member" as of the effective date of these By-Laws.

(2) **Membership Agreement:** The person has executed a membership agreement with the Corporation, whether in writing or by electronic means.

---

*These Amended and Restated By-Laws were duly adopted by the Governing Board of the University of Science and Technology of China Alumni Foundation on November 17, 2016.*
