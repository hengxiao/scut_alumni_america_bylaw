# CONSTITUTION AND BY-LAWS

## OF

## OCA — ASIAN PACIFIC AMERICAN ADVOCATES

*(Formerly: Organization of Chinese Americans)*

*A District of Columbia Nonprofit Corporation — IRS 501(c)(3)*

*Originally Adopted: June 9, 1973 | Amended: 2013*

---

## ARTICLE I — Name, Mission, and Legal Status

**Section 1.01 — Name.** The name of this organization is **OCA – Asian Pacific American Advocates** (hereinafter "OCA" or the "National Organization"). Prior to 2013, the organization was known as the Organization of Chinese Americans.

**Section 1.02 — Mission.** The mission of OCA is to advance the social, political, and economic well-being of Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders (AAPIs) in the United States.

**Section 1.03 — Legal Status.** OCA National is incorporated as a nonprofit corporation in the District of Columbia and has held IRS 501(c)(3) tax-exempt status. Donations to OCA are tax-deductible to the extent permitted by applicable law.

**Section 1.04 — Principal Office.** The principal office of OCA National shall be located in Washington, D.C. The Board of Directors may change the address as needed.

**Section 1.05 — Non-Partisan Character.** OCA is a non-partisan organization. OCA shall not endorse, support, or oppose any candidate for political office, but may engage in civic education and advocacy on issues of concern to the AAPI community.

---

## ARTICLE II — Purposes

**Section 2.01.** The specific purposes of OCA are:

(a) To advocate for the civil rights, equal opportunity, and political representation of Asian Americans and Pacific Islanders;

(b) To provide leadership development programs for AAPI youth and emerging leaders;

(c) To support professional and career development of AAPI community members;

(d) To promote voter education and civic participation among AAPIs;

(e) To combat anti-Asian discrimination, hate crimes, and inaccurate media portrayals;

(f) To advocate for immigration reform that serves the interests of AAPI communities;

(g) To promote educational access and scholarship for AAPIs;

(h) To foster mutual understanding between AAPI communities and other communities in the United States;

(i) To coordinate with and support OCA's chapters and affiliates across the United States.

---

## ARTICLE III — Membership

**Section 3.01 — Individual Membership.** Individual membership in OCA shall be open to any person who subscribes to OCA's mission and pays the applicable annual dues. Individual members shall be affiliated with a local chapter if one exists in their area.

**Section 3.02 — Chapter Membership.** OCA's network of over fifty (50) chapters and affiliates across the United States shall constitute organizational members of the National Organization. Chapters are independent nonprofits that affiliate with OCA National under a charter agreement.

**Section 3.03 — Dues.** Individual and chapter dues shall be set by the Board of Directors. Dues shall reflect the organization's commitment to accessibility, with student and low-income membership categories available.

**Section 3.04 — Voting Rights.** Individual members in good standing may vote at the Annual National Convention. Chapters are represented at the Convention by delegates elected or appointed by each chapter.

---

## ARTICLE IV — National Convention

**Section 4.01 — Authority.** The Annual National Convention is the supreme governing body of OCA. The Convention shall have authority to: (i) elect national officers and the Board of Directors; (ii) adopt policy resolutions; (iii) amend this Constitution and By-Laws; and (iv) receive reports from the Board, committees, and chapters.

**Section 4.02 — Frequency.** The National Convention shall be held annually, generally in the summer months. The date, location, and host chapter shall be determined by the Board of Directors.

**Section 4.03 — Delegates.** Each chapter shall be entitled to send delegates to the National Convention in proportion to its membership, as specified in the chapter charter agreement. Individual at-large members may also attend and vote as delegates under rules established by the Board.

**Section 4.04 — Notice.** Notice of the National Convention shall be provided to all chapters and individual members at least sixty (60) days in advance.

**Section 4.05 — Quorum.** A quorum for the National Convention shall consist of delegates representing at least twenty (20) chapters.

---

## ARTICLE V — Board of Directors

**Section 5.01 — Powers.** Between National Conventions, the Board of Directors (the "Board") shall govern OCA's affairs, manage the National Organization, and carry out the mandates of the Convention.

**Section 5.02 — Composition.** The Board shall consist of: (i) elected national officers; (ii) chapter representatives elected at the National Convention; and (iii) such at-large directors as the Convention may determine, provided the total Board membership shall not exceed twenty-five (25) persons.

**Section 5.03 — Terms.** Board members shall serve two-year terms between Conventions.

**Section 5.04 — Meetings.** The Board shall meet at least three (3) times per year. A special meeting may be called by the President or by any five (5) Directors. Notice shall be provided at least fourteen (14) days in advance. Meetings may be held by video conference.

**Section 5.05 — Quorum and Voting.** A quorum shall consist of a majority of Directors then serving. Actions shall require a majority vote of Directors present at a duly held meeting, except where a higher vote is required by this Constitution.

---

## ARTICLE VI — National Officers

**Section 6.01 — Officers.** The national officers of OCA shall be: President, First Vice President, Second Vice President, Secretary, and Treasurer.

**Section 6.02 — Election.** National officers shall be elected by the delegates at the Annual National Convention. Candidates must be individual members in good standing. Election shall require a majority of votes cast. If no candidate receives a majority, a runoff shall be held between the top two candidates.

**Section 6.03 — Terms.** National officers shall serve two-year terms. No officer may serve more than two consecutive terms in the same office.

**Section 6.04 — President.** The President shall:

(a) Serve as the chief spokesperson and public representative of OCA;

(b) Preside at all National Convention sessions and Board meetings;

(c) Implement the policies adopted by the Convention and the Board;

(d) Supervise the Executive Director and national staff;

(e) Appoint committee chairs with Board approval.

**Section 6.05 — Vice Presidents.** The First Vice President shall assist the President and act as President in the President's absence. The Second Vice President shall oversee chapter relations and development.

**Section 6.06 — Secretary.** The Secretary shall keep records of all Convention sessions and Board meetings, maintain the official membership roll, and manage official communications.

**Section 6.07 — Treasurer.** The Treasurer shall oversee the financial affairs of OCA National, present financial reports to the Board and to the Convention, and co-sign financial transactions above a threshold set by the Board.

---

## ARTICLE VII — Executive Director

**Section 7.01.** The Board shall appoint an Executive Director to serve as the chief professional staff officer of OCA National. The Executive Director shall be responsible for day-to-day management of staff, programs, and operations under the direction of the Board and the President.

**Section 7.02.** The Executive Director shall not be a voting member of the Board but shall report to and be supervised by the Board.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — Chapters

**Section 8.01 — Chartering.** A group of at least fifteen (15) individual OCA members in a geographic area may petition the Board for charter recognition as a chapter of OCA. The Board shall evaluate the petition and may grant, deny, or conditionally approve the charter.

**Section 8.02 — Chapter Obligations.** Chartered chapters shall:

(a) Operate in accordance with OCA's mission, constitution, and policies;

(b) Pay annual chapter dues to OCA National;

(c) Send delegates to the Annual National Convention;

(d) Submit annual activity and financial reports to OCA National;

(e) Comply with OCA's non-discrimination policies.

**Section 8.03 — Chapter Independence.** Within the constraints of OCA's national framework, chapters shall have autonomy to adopt their own by-laws, elect their own officers, and manage their own programs and finances. Chapter by-laws shall not conflict with this Constitution.

**Section 8.04 — Revocation.** The Board may revoke a chapter charter for cause, including conduct inconsistent with OCA's mission or non-compliance with chapter obligations, by a two-thirds (2/3) vote.

---

## ARTICLE IX — Finance

**Section 9.01.** All funds of OCA National shall be held in accounts in the name of OCA in one or more federally insured financial institutions.

**Section 9.02.** The Board shall approve an annual operating budget. Expenditures shall be made in accordance with the approved budget.

**Section 9.03.** The Treasurer shall present a financial report at each Board meeting and a comprehensive annual report at the National Convention.

**Section 9.04.** OCA National shall engage an independent certified public accountant to conduct an annual audit of the organization's financial statements.

**Section 9.05.** The fiscal year shall end on December 31 of each year.

---

## ARTICLE X — Amendments

**Section 10.01.** This Constitution and By-Laws may be amended at any Annual National Convention by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the delegates present and voting.

**Section 10.02.** Proposed amendments must be submitted to the Board at least ninety (90) days before the Convention. The Board shall circulate all proposed amendments to all chapters and individual members at least forty-five (45) days before the Convention.

**Section 10.03.** Emergency amendments between Conventions may be adopted by a three-fourths (3/4) vote of the full Board, subject to ratification at the next Convention.

---

## ARTICLE XI — Dissolution

Upon dissolution of OCA National, after payment of all debts and liabilities, all remaining assets shall be distributed to organizations qualifying as tax-exempt under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, with preference for organizations serving the AAPI community or civil rights purposes, as determined by the Board.

---

*This Constitution and By-Laws of OCA – Asian Pacific American Advocates was originally adopted on June 9, 1973 and has been amended from time to time, including the 2013 rebranding from "Organization of Chinese Americans" to "OCA – Asian Pacific American Advocates."*
