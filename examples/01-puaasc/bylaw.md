# BY-LAWS

## OF

## PEKING UNIVERSITY ALUMNI ASSOCIATION OF SOUTHERN CALIFORNIA

*A California Nonprofit Mutual Benefit Corporation*

---

## ARTICLE I — Name

**Section 1.** The name of this corporation is the **Peking University Alumni Association of Southern California** (hereinafter referred to as the "Association" or "PUAASC").

**Section 2.** The Association is incorporated in the State of California as a Nonprofit Mutual Benefit Corporation under the California Nonprofit Corporation Law, Part 3 (commencing with Section 7110).

---

## ARTICLE II — Principal Office

**Section 1.** The principal office of the Association shall be located in the County of Los Angeles, State of California. The Board of Directors shall have authority to change the location of the principal office as deemed necessary.

---

## ARTICLE III — Purposes

**Section 1.** The purposes of this Association are:

(a) To promote friendship, camaraderie, and mutual support among alumni of Peking University residing in Southern California;

(b) To facilitate professional networking and career development among members;

(c) To promote academic and intellectual exchange between the Southern California community and Peking University;

(d) To support Peking University's educational mission through advocacy and fundraising activities;

(e) To preserve and promote Chinese cultural heritage in the Southern California Chinese-American community;

(f) To serve as a liaison between Peking University and its alumni in Southern California.

**Section 2.** The Association is not organized for profit, and no part of its assets shall inure to the benefit of any member or private individual. Members shall not be compensated for services except for expenses approved by the Board of Directors.

**Section 3.** The Association shall not participate in any partisan political activities or engage in any activities that would jeopardize its nonprofit status.

---

## ARTICLE IV — Membership

**Section 1: Qualifications**

Any individual who graduated from, or was formerly employed as faculty or staff at, Peking University, and who currently resides, works, or studies in the Southern California area (including Los Angeles, Orange, San Diego, Riverside, San Bernardino, and Ventura Counties), may become a member of this Association.

Honorary membership may be conferred by the Board of Directors upon any individual who has made outstanding contributions to the Association or to Peking University. Honorary members are not entitled to vote.

**Section 2: Rights, Privileges, and Obligations**

(a) Each regular member in good standing has the right to vote, to elect, and to be elected to the Board of Directors.

(b) All members in good standing may participate in all activities and programs sponsored or organized by the Association.

(c) All members shall abide by these By-Laws and by the decisions and directives of the Board of Directors.

**Section 3: Membership Dues**

Each member shall pay annual membership dues in an amount determined by the Board of Directors from time to time. A life membership option may be established by the Board of Directors. The Board may set different dues levels for student members.

**Section 4: Membership Meetings**

(a) A regular annual membership meeting shall be held once each year. Special membership meetings may be called by the Chairman or by a majority vote of the Board of Directors.

(b) Notice of all membership meetings shall be served by the Secretary at least fourteen (14) days prior to the date of the meeting, by written notice or electronic mail, stating the purpose, date, time, and location.

(c) The agenda of the annual membership meeting shall include, but is not limited to: election of Directors; presentation of the annual financial report; review and discussion of the Association's activities in the past year; and discussion of plans and proposals for the coming year.

---

## ARTICLE V — Board of Directors

**Section 1: Powers**

The governing powers of the Association shall be vested in a Board of Directors (hereinafter the "Board"). Subject to the limitations of the California Nonprofit Corporation Law, these By-Laws, and any other applicable law, all corporate powers shall be exercised by or under the authority of the Board.

**Section 2: Number**

The Board shall consist of no fewer than nine (9) and no more than twenty-one (21) Directors. The Board may, by resolution adopted by a two-thirds majority, fix the authorized number of Directors within that range.

**Section 3: Duties**

The Board shall:

(a) Manage and oversee the activities of the Association consistent with these By-Laws;

(b) Cause accurate records of all Board meetings and financial transactions to be kept;

(c) Present a written annual financial report at the annual membership meeting, showing the assets and liabilities of the Association;

(d) Call annual and special membership meetings;

(e) Appoint and oversee executive officers;

(f) Plan activities and programs for the benefit of members;

(g) Undertake all actions necessary to fulfill the purposes of the Association.

**Section 4: Meetings**

(a) The Board shall hold at least two (2) regular meetings per year. Special meetings may be called at any time by the Chairman, any Vice Chairman, the Secretary, or by a written request signed by any four (4) Directors.

(b) Notice of Board meetings shall be provided by the Secretary at least seven (7) days prior to the meeting, except that special meetings called for urgent matters may be called upon forty-eight (48) hours' notice.

(c) A quorum shall consist of a majority of the authorized number of Directors. Actions of the Board shall require a majority vote of the Directors present at a duly held meeting at which a quorum is present, except where a higher vote is required by these By-Laws or by law.

(d) Minutes shall be kept of each Board meeting. A summary of each Board meeting shall be made accessible to Association members.

**Section 5: Term of Office**

Each Director shall hold office for a term of three (3) years, or until a successor is duly elected and qualified.

**Section 6: Nomination and Election**

(a) Any member in good standing may be nominated as a candidate for Director.

(b) Directors shall be elected by the members at the annual membership meeting. Each member shall have one vote per candidate. Candidates who receive a majority of votes cast are elected. If the number of candidates elected is fewer than the minimum required number of Directors, the candidates with the highest vote totals shall be elected until the minimum is reached.

(c) Voting may be conducted electronically. Ballots shall be open for a period specified by the Board.

**Section 7: Vacancies**

(a) A Director may resign by giving written notice to the Chairman, any Vice Chairman, or the Secretary. The resignation is effective upon receipt unless a future effective date is specified.

(b) A Director who is absent from three (3) consecutive Board meetings without excuse approved by the Board may be declared to have resigned.

(c) Vacancies on the Board shall be filled by appointment by the remaining Directors until the next annual election.

---

## ARTICLE VI — Officers

**Section 1: Composition and Term**

The officers of the Association shall consist of: one (1) Chairman, up to four (4) Vice Chairmen, one (1) Secretary, and one (1) Treasurer. Officers shall serve for a term of three (3) years, concurrent with the Board term in which they are elected or appointed.

**Section 2: Chairman**

(a) The Chairman shall be the chief executive officer of the Association.

(b) The Chairman shall preside at all meetings of the Board and of the membership.

(c) The Chairman shall execute all directives and policies established by the Board and shall represent the Association in external relations.

(d) The Chairman shall be elected by the Board of Directors. A candidate must receive a majority of votes cast to be elected. If no candidate receives a majority in the first round, a runoff shall be held between the top two vote-getters; the candidate receiving the greater number of votes in the runoff shall be elected. Ties shall be resolved by lot.

**Section 3: Vice Chairmen**

Vice Chairmen shall assist the Chairman in the discharge of duties and shall exercise the Chairman's authority when the Chairman is absent or unable to serve. Vice Chairmen shall be elected by the Board of Directors by the same process as the Chairman.

**Section 4: Secretary**

The Secretary shall:

(a) Keep full and accurate minutes of all Board and membership meetings;

(b) Provide required notices of meetings to Directors and members;

(c) Maintain official records and correspondence of the Association;

(d) Perform such other duties as the Board may assign.

The Secretary shall be appointed by the Board of Directors for a term of three (3) years.

**Section 5: Treasurer**

The Treasurer shall:

(a) Collect, receive, and account for all dues, donations, and other income of the Association;

(b) Maintain accurate accounts of all assets and liabilities and of all financial transactions;

(c) Co-sign checks and disbursements as authorized by the Board;

(d) Submit an annual financial statement to the Board and to the membership;

(e) Serve as custodian of all financial records and accounts.

The Treasurer shall be appointed by the Board of Directors for a term of three (3) years.

---

## ARTICLE VII — Finances

**Section 1.** All funds of the Association shall be deposited in one or more federally insured financial institutions in accounts held in the name of the Peking University Alumni Association of Southern California.

**Section 2.** Checks, drafts, or other withdrawals of funds shall require the signatures of at least two (2) authorized officers.

**Section 3.** The fiscal year of the Association shall be the calendar year, unless otherwise determined by the Board.

**Section 4.** The Treasurer shall present an annual financial report to the Board and to the membership at the annual membership meeting.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — Committees

**Section 1.** The Board may establish standing or special committees to assist in carrying out the Association's purposes. Each committee shall report to and be accountable to the Board.

**Section 2.** Standing committees may include, but are not limited to: Events and Activities Committee, Membership Committee, Finance Committee, Scholarship Committee, and Communications Committee.

**Section 3.** Committee members need not be Directors but must be members in good standing of the Association.

---

## ARTICLE IX — Indemnification

The Association shall indemnify its Directors and officers to the fullest extent permitted by California law against all expenses, liabilities, and losses reasonably incurred in connection with any action, suit, or proceeding arising from the person's role as a Director or officer, provided that the person acted in good faith and in a manner the person reasonably believed to be in the best interests of the Association.

---

## ARTICLE X — Amendments to By-Laws

**Section 1: Proposal**

These By-Laws may be amended, revised, or repealed by the following process. An amendment shall be proposed in writing and submitted by: (a) any six (6) or more Directors; or (b) at least ten percent (10%) of the members in good standing.

**Section 2: Notice**

A proposed amendment shall be announced to all members at least thirty (30) days prior to the date of the annual membership meeting at which it will be voted upon.

**Section 3: Adoption**

An amendment shall be adopted by a two-thirds (2/3) majority vote of the members present at the membership meeting at which the amendment is presented, provided a quorum is present.

---

## ARTICLE XI — Dissolution

In the event of dissolution of the Association, after payment of all debts and liabilities, all remaining assets shall be distributed to one or more nonprofit organizations that carry on purposes substantially similar to those of this Association, as determined by the Board of Directors, and no part of the net assets shall inure to the benefit of any member or private individual.

---

*These By-Laws were duly adopted by the Board of Directors of the Peking University Alumni Association of Southern California.*
