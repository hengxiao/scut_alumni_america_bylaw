# BY-LAWS

## OF

## FUDAN UNIVERSITY ALUMNI ASSOCIATION OF GREATER LOS ANGELES

*An Independent California Nonprofit Organization*

---

## ARTICLE I — Name

**Section 1.** The name of this organization is the **Fudan University Alumni Association of Greater Los Angeles** (hereinafter referred to as "FDAAGLA" or the "Association").

**Section 2.** The Association is an independent, non-profit, and non-political organization. It is not affiliated with or controlled by any government, political party, or other external entity.

---

## ARTICLE II — Geographic Scope

**Section 1.** For purposes of these By-Laws, the **Greater Los Angeles Area** is defined to include the five counties of Southern California: Los Angeles, Orange, Riverside, San Bernardino, and Ventura.

**Section 2.** The Board of Directors may, by resolution, extend or modify the geographic scope of the Association in response to membership needs.

---

## ARTICLE III — Purposes

**Section 1.** The purposes of FDAAGLA are:

(a) To promote friendship, networking, and mutual benefit among Association members;

(b) To provide assistance and support to members in need;

(c) To facilitate information exchange between members and Fudan University;

(d) To encourage educational exchange and cultural understanding between the United States and China;

(e) To organize and support scholarly and scientific forums, seminars, and events;

(f) To support Fudan University's educational and research missions.

**Section 2.** No part of the assets of the Association shall inure to the benefit of any individual member. The Association shall not engage in partisan political activities.

---

## ARTICLE IV — Membership

**Section 1: Categories of Membership**

FDAAGLA shall have the following categories of membership:

(a) **General Members:** Individuals who have studied at Fudan University, the Shanghai Medical University (SMU, prior to its merger with Fudan), or any predecessor institution for at least two (2) years, **and** who have resided in the Greater Los Angeles Area for at least one (1) year prior to applying for membership. General members hold full voting rights.

(b) **Associate Members:** Spouses, domestic partners, or immediate family members of General Members, or individuals with a significant connection to the Fudan community as determined by the Board. Associate members may participate in all Association activities but do not hold voting rights.

(c) **Honorary Members:** Individuals who have made a significant contribution to Fudan University or to FDAAGLA, nominated by any Director and approved by a two-thirds (2/3) majority of the Board. Honorary members do not hold voting rights and are not required to pay dues.

**Section 2: Membership Dues**

(a) Each General Member and Associate Member shall pay annual membership dues of twenty dollars ($20), or a one-time lifetime membership fee of two hundred dollars ($200).

(b) A General Member who holds a valid student identification card from any accredited college or university is entitled to free membership.

(c) Dues shall be due and payable by January 31 of each year. Members who have not paid dues by March 31 shall be considered not in good standing and may not vote until dues are paid.

**Section 3: Voting**

(a) Each General Member in good standing is entitled to one (1) vote at any membership meeting.

(b) Voting shall be conducted in person only. Voting by proxy is not permitted.

(c) Honorary members and Associate Members may not vote.

**Section 4: Annual Membership Meeting**

(a) An annual membership meeting shall be held once each year. The date, time, and location shall be determined by the Board and communicated to members at least twenty-one (21) days in advance.

(b) The agenda shall include: report of the Board; financial report; election of Directors; and any other business properly before the membership.

**Section 5: Special Meetings**

Special meetings of the membership may be called by the President, by a majority of the Board, or by written petition of at least fifteen percent (15%) of the General Members in good standing.

---

## ARTICLE V — Board of Directors

**Section 1: Powers**

The Board of Directors (the "Board") shall govern and manage the activities, finances, and affairs of FDAAGLA, subject to these By-Laws and applicable California law.

**Section 2: Number**

The Board shall consist of no fewer than five (5) and no more than fifteen (15) Directors. All Directors must be General Members in good standing.

**Section 3: Terms and Election**

(a) Directors shall serve terms of two (2) years.

(b) Directors shall be elected by the General Members at the annual membership meeting by a plurality of votes cast.

(c) Directors may serve a maximum of three (3) consecutive terms, after which they must take at least one (1) term off before being eligible for re-election.

**Section 4: Duties**

The Board shall:

(a) Carry out and advance the purposes of FDAAGLA;

(b) Approve the annual budget and financial reports;

(c) Appoint and oversee officers;

(d) Establish committees as needed;

(e) Present an annual report to the membership;

(f) Ensure the legal and financial integrity of the Association.

**Section 5: Compensation**

Directors shall serve without compensation except that they may be reimbursed for their actual and necessary expenses incurred in performing their duties, as approved by a majority of the Board.

**Section 6: Meetings**

(a) The Board shall hold at least four (4) regular meetings per year.

(b) Special meetings may be called by the President or by any three (3) Directors upon at least five (5) days' written notice.

(c) A quorum shall consist of a majority of the Directors then serving. Actions shall require a majority vote of Directors present at a meeting with quorum.

(d) The Board may hold meetings by conference call, video conference, or other electronic means, with full voting rights.

**Section 7: Removal and Vacancies**

(a) A Director may be removed for cause by a two-thirds (2/3) vote of the remaining Directors.

(b) Vacancies shall be filled by appointment by the remaining Directors until the next annual election.

---

## ARTICLE VI — Officers

**Section 1: Composition**

The officers of FDAAGLA shall be: President, Vice President(s) (up to three), Secretary, and Treasurer. Officers shall be elected from among and by the Board of Directors.

**Section 2: President**

(a) The President shall serve as the chief executive of the Association.

(b) The President shall preside at all meetings of the Board and of the membership.

(c) The President shall execute the policies and decisions of the Board and represent the Association externally.

**Section 3: Vice Presidents**

Vice Presidents shall assist the President and act as President in the President's absence.

**Section 4: Secretary**

The Secretary shall keep records, minutes, and official communications of the Association and shall provide required notices to Directors and members.

**Section 5: Treasurer**

The Treasurer shall maintain financial records, prepare financial reports, and oversee banking and financial transactions of the Association.

**Section 6: Terms**

Officers shall serve for a term of two (2) years concurrent with their Board term.

---

## ARTICLE VII — Finances

**Section 1.** All Association funds shall be deposited in accounts held in the name of "Fudan University Alumni Association of Greater Los Angeles" at one or more federally insured financial institutions.

**Section 2.** Disbursements shall require the signatures of at least two (2) authorized officers.

**Section 3.** The Treasurer shall prepare and present an annual financial statement to the Board and to the membership at the annual meeting.

**Section 4.** The fiscal year of the Association shall end on December 31 of each year.

---

## ARTICLE VIII — Committees

**Section 1.** The Board may establish standing and special committees to advance the purposes of the Association. Committees shall report to and be accountable to the Board.

**Section 2.** All members in good standing are eligible to serve on committees.

---

## ARTICLE IX — Amendments

**Section 1.** These By-Laws may be amended as follows:

(a) A proposed amendment must first be approved by a simple majority of the Board of Directors at a duly held Board meeting; and

(b) The amendment must then be ratified by a simple majority of the General Members in good standing at the next annual membership meeting.

**Section 2.** Proposed amendments must be circulated to all members at least twenty-one (21) days before the membership meeting at which the ratification vote will occur.

---

## ARTICLE X — Dissolution

Upon dissolution of FDAAGLA, after payment of all debts and obligations, the remaining assets shall be distributed to one or more nonprofit organizations whose purposes are substantially similar to those of FDAAGLA, as determined by the Board, and in no case shall any assets be distributed to any member or private individual.

---

*These By-Laws were adopted by the Board of Directors of the Fudan University Alumni Association of Greater Los Angeles.*
