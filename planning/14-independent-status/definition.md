# Topic: Independent Status and Organizational Autonomy

## Definition

This topic establishes that SCUT Alumni America is an independent, U.S.-based nonprofit organization that operates solely under U.S. law and the authority of its own members and elected leadership. It answers:

- Is the organization independent from South China University of Technology (SCUT), the Chinese government, the Chinese Communist Party (CCP), and any foreign embassy, consulate, or governmental body?
- Does any foreign government entity have the right to direct, guide, approve, or veto the organization's activities, decisions, or leadership selections?
- Are membership eligibility, member benefits, or member obligations in any way conditioned on loyalty or deference to a foreign government or institution?
- Does the organization collect, retain, or disclose member information to any foreign government entity?
- How does the organization describe its relationship with SCUT in a way that reflects genuine alumni affinity without creating a governance dependency?
- What financial boundaries ensure that the organization is not financially dependent on or directed by any foreign government or institution?
- How does the organization respond if a foreign government entity attempts to assert influence over its governance, activities, or communications?

## Why This Matters

### The Regulatory and Political Environment (2026)

In March 2026, U.S. Congress members (House Select Committee on China Chairman John Moolenaar, House Foreign Affairs Committee Chairman Brian Mast, and House Education and Workforce Committee Chairman Tim Walberg) formally asked Secretary of State Marco Rubio to designate the Chinese Students and Scholars Association (CSSA) as a foreign mission of the People's Republic of China. Their letter cited:

- CSSA constitutions in some chapters expressly stating the organization "accepts the guidance" of the Chinese consulate-general.
- The Chinese Embassy asserting approval authority over CSSA leadership candidates.
- Membership provisions amounting to loyalty pledges to Chinese government policy objectives.
- Evidence that CSSAs have been used to monitor and report on Chinese students and scholars abroad.

Although SCUT Alumni America is an alumni association — not a student organization — the scrutiny applied to CSSA chapters applies equally to any organization with a name-based connection to a Chinese institution. An organization whose bylaws do not explicitly address its independence is exposed to:

1. **Regulatory risk:** Potential designation as a foreign mission under the Foreign Missions Act (22 U.S.C. § 4301 et seq.), or registration requirements under the Foreign Agents Registration Act (FARA, 22 U.S.C. § 611 et seq.).
2. **Legal liability:** Officers and directors of an organization that accepts direction from a foreign government without FARA registration may face criminal penalties.
3. **Reputational harm:** Members who are U.S. citizens, permanent residents, or hold security clearances may face professional consequences if the organization is perceived as a foreign-influence vehicle.
4. **Institutional relationships:** Universities, government agencies, and U.S.-based corporations may decline to partner with or recognize an organization that lacks explicit independence provisions.

### The Relationship With SCUT

The current By-Laws (Article III §1(d)–(e)) commit the organization to "enhance the connection between the members and South China University of Technology" and "help and support the development of South China University of Technology." These are legitimate expressions of alumni affinity. However, without explicit independence provisions, they could be read as creating an institutional dependency that subjects the organization to SCUT's direction — and, by extension, to the direction of the Chinese Ministry of Education or other Chinese governmental bodies that oversee Chinese universities.

The bylaws must clearly distinguish between:
- **Alumni affinity** (celebrating a shared educational experience, maintaining social connections, promoting professional networking among graduates): Legitimate and fully compatible with U.S. law and independent status.
- **Institutional dependency** (accepting operational guidance from SCUT, operating as a de facto extension of SCUT's overseas operations, subordinating organizational decisions to SCUT preferences): Incompatible with independent status and potentially subject to FARA.
